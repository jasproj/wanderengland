About London

London is the largest market on this site, with more than 130 listed tours covering everything from blue-badge guided walks of the City and Westminster to themed private tours (Doctor Who, Jack the Ripper, Harry Potter filming locations, the women's history of the City). The strongest operators we work with are the small-group private outfits — companies like London Guided Walks Private Tours and London Walks — rather than the big bus-tour operators, which have their own booking infrastructure outside FareHarbor.

If you've never been to London before, the standard first-day itinerary is a 2-hour Westminster walking tour (Big Ben, Westminster Abbey, the Houses of Parliament) followed by an afternoon at the British Museum. For a second visit, the East End walks (Spitalfields, Whitechapel, Brick Lane) and the Maritime Greenwich half-day are stronger picks. London tours are mostly weather-flexible — operators reschedule for heavy rain — and run year-round, with the highest density of departures from May through September.

What to know before you book

  • Highest tour density on the site — 130+ listed across walking, themed, and private formats
  • Best months: May-September (long daylight, festival season). Worst: late January-February
  • Most walking tours include 4-6 km of walking on uneven streets — wear comfortable shoes
  • Private themed tours (Doctor Who, Harry Potter, Jack the Ripper) typically run 2-3 hours
  • Tube + walking is faster than taxi for most central London tour pickups
  • Free reschedule policies vary — confirm 24-72 hour windows on the operator page
