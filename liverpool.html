Liverpool Tours

Beatles heritage, the Albert Dock, and one of England's most-walked maritime city tours.

About Liverpool

Liverpool's tour market is dominated by Beatles heritage walks — Mathew Street, the Cavern Club, Penny Lane, Strawberry Field — but the city has more than that. The Royal Albert Dock (UNESCO World Heritage) is a 19th-century wet dock complex now full of museums (Tate Liverpool, the International Slavery Museum, Maritime Museum) and waterfront restaurants. The Liverpool Cathedral (Anglican) and the Metropolitan Cathedral (Catholic) are at opposite ends of Hope Street, both in walking distance of city center.

Most Liverpool tours we work with are 2-3 hour walking tours with private and shared formats. If you have a half-day, the standard pairing is a Beatles morning walk + Albert Dock afternoon. Football fans should check the Anfield (Liverpool FC) or Goodison Park (Everton) stadium tours, which are booked separately by the clubs but listed alongside our partners.

What to know before you book

  • Beatles heritage walking tours are the strongest category — book 1 week ahead in summer
  • Royal Albert Dock is UNESCO-listed and has 4 museums plus waterfront food
  • Liverpool Cathedral (Anglican) is the largest cathedral in Britain; the Metropolitan (Catholic) is striking and modern — both worth the walk
  • Hope Street connects the two cathedrals — about 10 minutes end to end
  • Football-stadium tours are booked through the clubs, not our partners
  • Best months: April-September; many tours run year-round
