Cambridge Tours

Punting on the Backs, King's College Chapel, and a slightly slower-paced rival to Oxford.

About Cambridge

Cambridge is a 50-minute train ride from London King's Cross. The University of Cambridge has 31 colleges (vs Oxford's 38), with most of the famous ones — King's, Trinity, St. John's, Queens' — sitting along the River Cam in a stretch known as 'the Backs' that you can punt past in a single 45-minute trip. King's College Chapel, with its Tudor fan-vaulted ceiling, is the most-photographed building in the city.

The best Cambridge half-day is the standard pattern: a 90-minute punting tour past the colleges (lots of operators on Quayside or Mill Lane), then a walking tour of the central colleges, then a wander to the Fitzwilliam Museum (free entry, world-class collection). Cambridge feels smaller and more compact than Oxford — most things are within a 15-minute walk of Market Square. The market itself is open daily and is good for lunch.

What to know before you book

  • 50 minutes by train from London King's Cross — slightly faster than Oxford
  • 31 colleges; King's, Trinity, St. John's, and Queens' are the must-sees
  • King's College Chapel is the most-photographed building in the city
  • Punting on the Cam runs year-round, but May-September is the prime season
  • The Fitzwilliam Museum has a world-class collection and is free
  • Walkable city center — most colleges within 10 min of Market Square
