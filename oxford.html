Oxford Tours

The 38 colleges of Oxford University, plus river punting on the Cherwell. Easy day-trip from London.

About Oxford

Oxford is a 1-hour train ride from London Paddington, which is why most of the tour bookings on this site are day-trips. The University of Oxford is made up of 38 individual colleges scattered across the city center, several of which double as filming locations (Christ Church for Harry Potter's Great Hall, Bodleian Library for the Hogwarts library). The standard tour format is a 2-hour walking tour that visits 3-4 colleges plus the Bodleian, Radcliffe Camera, and the Sheldonian Theatre.

Punting on the River Cherwell — a flat-bottomed boat propelled with a long pole — is the other classic Oxford activity. Magdalen Bridge Boathouse (May-September) and Cherwell Boathouse rent for self-punt or guided trips. Most of our partners offer combined walking + punting half-days, which is the best one-day Oxford pattern. Avoid mid-June for matriculation/exam-season closures of some colleges; September is the sweet spot.

What to know before you book

  • 1 hour by train from London Paddington — easiest UK university day-trip
  • 38 colleges, but most tours visit 3-4 (typically Christ Church, New College, Magdalen)
  • Punting season runs May through September; off-season tours are walking-only
  • College access can be restricted during exam periods (May-June) — confirm on tour page
  • Combined walking + punting half-days (4 hrs) are the best one-day pattern
  • Most tours start at the Bodleian Library or Radcliffe Camera — easy meeting points
